1. Overview & Key Principles

The Cobblestone Pub booking system is a web application that manages all Backroom and Upstairs bookings in one place. It replaces the spreadsheet and email back-and-forth that the team used previously.

What the system does for you automatically

  • Sends an auto-acknowledgement email to the band the moment they submit an inquiry
  • Gives every booking a private portal URL the band can bookmark to check their status
  • Sends a full confirmation email (with a calendar invite) when you click Confirm
  • Adds/removes events from the Google Calendar automatically on confirm and cancel
  • Sends a reminder email to the band 3 days before their gig
  • Blocks confirmed and tentative dates on the public availability calendar so new inquiries can't land on a taken date

What you still do manually

  • Review inquiries and decide whether to confirm, hold as tentative, or decline
  • Create the Squarespace event listing (the system generates the copy — you paste it in)
  • Tick "Published on Squarespace" once the listing is live
  • Mark fees as paid once money has changed hands on the night
⚠️ Golden rule — everything goes through the system

Never add a booking directly to Google Calendar. If something was booked over the phone or by email, enter it in the system at cobblestone-pub.onrender.com/bookings/new first. Google Calendar is a display screen — the webapp is the source of truth.

4. Handling a New Inquiry

4a. Inquiry submitted via the website form

When a band finds the booking form at cobblestonepub.ie/book and fills it in, the system handles the first step for you:

  • A new booking row appears in the tracker with status Inquiry
  • The band automatically receives an acknowledgement email with their portal link
  • The "Inquiries to review" counter on the Bookings page goes up by one

You will not receive an email notification automatically — make it a habit to check the Bookings tracker at the start of each day.

4b. Inquiry by email, phone call, or walk-in

If someone contacts the pub directly rather than using the form, you need to enter the booking manually:

  1. Go to Bookings → Add Booking (top right button on the bookings page).
  2. Fill in everything you know: act name, contact details, date, event type, venue.
  3. Set Source to Manual and Status to Inquiry or Tentative depending on how solid the conversation was.
  4. Save. The band will not automatically receive an email at this stage — you entered it manually, so it's assumed you're still in conversation.
  5. When you're ready to send them their portal link, open the booking detail page and click Send Portal Link in the sidebar. This sends a short email with their personal URL.

Reviewing an inquiry

Once you've reviewed the details, you have three options:

DecisionWhat to do
Looks good — hold the date Open the booking, click Mark Tentative. Date turns amber on public calendar. Reply to the band to let them know you're checking details.
Ready to commit Go straight to Section 5 — Confirming.
Date not available or doesn't suit Click Cancel Booking on the detail page. Add a note explaining why. Email the band using the Message Band box to let them know and suggest alternatives if possible.
Aim to respond within 2–3 working days

The auto-ack email tells bands to expect a response within 2–3 working days. The "Inquiries to review" counter on the dashboard is your prompt — if it's not zero, someone needs attention.

5. Confirming a Booking

Confirming is a single button click — the system does the rest.

  1. Open the booking detail page (click the act name from the tracker, or the booking ID).
  2. In the Quick Actions sidebar, review the booking details — make sure date, venue, times, and contact email are correct. Fill in door time and start time if you have them.
  3. Click Confirm & Email Band.

The system then does all of this automatically:

  • Sets status to Confirmed
  • Sends the band a full confirmation email with: booking summary, "Good to Know" info (venue fee, Shane's contact details, ticketing notes, access info), a calendar invite (.ics file), and their portal URL
  • Creates an event in the Google Calendar (Backroom or Upstairs calendar, depending on the venue)
  • Stamps the confirmation timestamp on the booking record
✓ After confirming — two things left to do
  1. Copy the Squarespace block and create the event listing (see Section 8).
  2. Check whether a door person has been requested — if so, the €50 door fee should be noted.
Missing times?

If you confirm a booking before door/start times are finalised, the confirmation email will say "TBC". That's fine — use the Message Band feature later to send the final times once agreed with Shane.

6. Communicating with Bands

Message Band — send a custom email from inside the system

On every booking detail page, there is a Message Band card in the right sidebar. Type a subject and message body and click Send — the email will be sent in the Cobblestone's branded template with the band's portal link automatically appended.

Use this for:

  • Confirming final door/start times after speaking with Shane
  • Asking for a poster or artist bio
  • Updating ticket/ticketing information
  • Any ad-hoc queries before the event

Every email sent through the system is logged in the Activity section at the bottom of the booking detail page — so anyone picking up the booking can see what's already been communicated.

Send Portal Link

Use this if a band has lost their confirmation email or asks for the link again. It sends a short email with their personal portal URL — it does not re-send the full confirmation.

What the band sees in their portal

When a band visits their portal URL (cobblestone-pub.onrender.com/book/[token]), they can:

  • See their booking status and all confirmed details
  • Upload a poster/artwork and an artist bio/press kit
  • Cancel their booking (sends an alert to you automatically)

8. Publishing on Squarespace

The system generates all the text you need for a Squarespace event listing. You copy and paste — no retyping.

  1. Open the booking detail page for a confirmed booking.
  2. In the right sidebar, find the Squarespace Block card. It contains a pre-formatted block with the event title, date, times, description, ticket info, and tags.
  3. Click Copy to clipboard.
  4. Go to Squarespace, create a new Event, and paste the content into the relevant fields.
  5. Add the poster image if the band has uploaded one (download it from the Files card on the detail page).
  6. Publish the Squarespace event.
  7. Back in the booking detail page, click Mark as published in the Fees & Listing card. This removes the booking from the "Need website listing" counter on the dashboard.
No description yet?

If the band hasn't provided a description when you confirm, the Squarespace block will be missing that section. Ask the band via Message Band, then update the Description field in the booking form and save. The Squarespace block regenerates automatically.

9. Reminders & Pre-Show Checklist

Automated 3-day reminder

The system automatically sends the band a reminder email 3 days before their event. It includes the date, times, and their portal link. You don't need to do anything — but it's worth knowing it goes out, so you don't accidentally send a duplicate.

Pre-show checklist (do a week before)

  • ☐ Door time and start time filled in on the booking
  • ☐ Confirmation that a door person is or isn't needed — tell Shane
  • ☐ Poster/artwork uploaded by the band (or chased if missing)
  • ☐ Squarespace event published and ticked
  • ☐ Ticketing link live (if Eventbrite — band manages this themselves)

10. Handling Cancellations

When the band cancels

Bands can cancel their own booking from their portal page. When they do:

  • An alert email is sent to the pub immediately with the band's reason
  • The booking is marked Cancelled (band) in the tracker
  • The Google Calendar event is automatically deleted
  • The band receives a cancellation confirmation email

No action is required from staff, but check the date is now free and decide whether to open it up again or add a blackout.

When you cancel on behalf of the pub

  1. Open the booking detail page.
  2. In Quick Actions, click Cancel Booking and confirm. Add a reason in the note field.
  3. Email the band using the Message Band feature to explain the cancellation and apologise.
Cancelling a confirmed booking

If a confirmed booking is cancelled, the Google Calendar event is automatically deleted and the date frees up on the public form. Always contact the band personally — cancelling in the system does not automatically send them an email explaining why.

11. Recurring Bookings — Classes & Series

For classes, weekly sessions, or any regularly repeating booking, use the Recurring Series feature rather than creating bookings one by one. A series generates all the individual booking dates at once.

Creating a new series

  1. From the Bookings page, click Recurring Series in the top button row.
  2. Click New Series.
  3. Fill in the act/class name, venue, event type, contact details, recurrence (weekly / every 2 weeks / monthly), start date, and end date.
  4. Add door time and start time if known.
  5. Click Create Series & Generate Bookings. The system creates one confirmed booking for each occurrence automatically.

Series bookings are set to Confirmed with no venue fee or door fee by default — appropriate for regular classes and sessions. If a specific occurrence has different terms, edit that individual booking from its detail page.

Cancelling some or all remaining dates

To cancel all upcoming dates in a series (e.g. a class is ending early):

  1. Go to Recurring Series and open the series.
  2. Click Cancel Remaining Dates in the sidebar.
  3. This cancels all future occurrences but leaves past dates untouched.

To cancel a single date within a series, open that individual booking from the series detail page and cancel it there.

12. Blackout Dates

A blackout date blocks a date on the public booking form so bands cannot submit an inquiry for it. Use this for:

  • Bank holidays when the venue is closed
  • Private hires booked directly (not through the system)
  • Maintenance days, staff events, or any date that needs to be kept clear

Adding a blackout

  1. From the Bookings page, click Blackout Dates.
  2. Choose the date, select whether it applies to Backroom only, Upstairs only, or both venues.
  3. Add a reason (optional but helpful for future reference).
  4. Click Add Blackout. The date immediately shows as unavailable on the public form.

Removing a blackout

On the Blackout Dates page, click the red trash icon next to the blackout. The date is freed up immediately.

Blackout dates are separate from bookings — they don't appear in the bookings tracker, just on the public availability calendar. If you want a full record (e.g. for a private hire with contact details), create a proper booking via Add Booking instead.

13. Band Contact List

The Band Contacts page (Bookings → Band Contacts) gives you a table of every unique act that has ever submitted an inquiry or booking, grouped by email address. It shows:

  • Act name and contact person
  • Email address and phone number
  • Total number of bookings and how many were confirmed
  • First and last booking dates
  • A quick "email" button that opens your email client, and a "view bookings" button to see their full history

This is useful for reaching out to bands you haven't heard from in a while, or for quickly looking up a contact without knowing which specific booking to open.
